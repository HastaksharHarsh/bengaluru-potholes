Silk Board, Bengaluru's Most Ambitious Obstacle Course. Silk Board Junction — a locality with a traffic density score of 99/100 and a rainfall of 42mm in the last 24 hours — has cemented its status as Bengaluru's undisputed pothole capital. With the highest concentration of critical severity potholes across all 10 monitored localities, BBMP's Ward 176 (Bommanahalli Zone) is now less a road and more a tactile navigation puzzle for the city's 99/100 traffic density commuters.

Civic data from the Bengaluru Road Watch system shows 157 potholes actively tracked across the city, of which Silk Board's cluster shows the highest severity scores — with the algorithm computing values of 80–100/100 for arterial roads under heavy load. A staggering ~85% of reported potholes remain unrepaired (only 15% ever cross the "repaired" threshold, per the 0.85 status roll in civic data). SLA breaches are rampant: potholes open for 18 days on a road with a 6-hour SLA for critical issues translates to a breach ratio of 72× over the allowed time. Contrast this with Jayanagar (traffic density: 72) where residents enjoy markedly fewer critical potholes and a more responsive ward engineer — proof that governance intent, not geography, separates disaster from livability.

Leading Solutions for a Better Future
1. AI-Powered Prediction (Live Now): The Road Watch system already flags "Critical Hotspot" clusters using geospatial progression analysis — potholes within 50m of each other get a shared risk score. Expanding this to trigger automatic work orders could cut response time by an estimated 40%.

2. Severity-Based SLA Enforcement: Current SLA = 6hrs for critical, yet 18-day-old craters exist. A real-time SLA breach alert dashboard directly to Eng. K. Murthy's phone (contact: +91 80 2573 8801) would be a start — assuming the engineer's phone doesn't also have a pothole in it.

3. Monsoon Pre-Treatment Program: With 42mm rainfall and Silk Board on the official monsoon risk zone list, pre-monsoon patching in April could prevent 70% of waterlogged craters. The data says so. The roads... agree silently.
